🐰 Your Bunny May Be Overweight

The information entered suggests your rabbit may weigh more than expected for its age and breed size.

What This Means

Some rabbits naturally have a larger build than others, so body condition is just as important as body weight. Extra body fat, however, can make it harder for rabbits to groom themselves, move comfortably, and may increase the risk of certain health problems.

Healthy Eating

A healthy rabbit's diet should be based primarily on unlimited grass hay, which supports digestion and dental health. Fresh water should always be available. Pellets should be fed in appropriate amounts, and treats such as fruit or commercial snacks should be offered only occasionally.

Exercise

Encourage daily exercise by providing a safe area where your rabbit can hop, explore, and play. Toys, tunnels, and cardboard boxes can help keep rabbits active and mentally stimulated.

Weight Management

Weight loss should happen gradually and under veterinary guidance if needed. Do not suddenly restrict food, because rabbits need to keep eating regularly to maintain a healthy digestive system.

When to Contact a Veterinarian

If you're concerned about your rabbit's weight or body condition, consult a rabbit-experienced veterinarian. They can assess your rabbit's overall health and recommend a safe nutrition and exercise plan if weight loss is appropriate.
