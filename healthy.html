Overview

Based on the information you entered, your rabbit's weight falls within a healthy range for its age group and general breed size. While this is encouraging, remember that body condition is just as important as body weight. Two rabbits can weigh the same but have different levels of body fat and muscle.

How to Keep Your Bunny Healthy

Continue providing unlimited fresh grass hay, which should make up the largest part of a healthy rabbit's diet. Fresh, clean water should always be available. Feed measured rabbit pellets according to your veterinarian's recommendations, and provide a variety of rabbit-safe leafy greens every day.

Exercise

Rabbits need plenty of room to hop, stretch, and explore every day. Daily exercise helps maintain a healthy body weight, supports muscle strength, and keeps your bunny mentally stimulated.

Regular Checkups

Weigh your rabbit regularly using the same scale whenever possible. If you notice sudden weight gain or weight loss, reduced appetite, or changes in behavior, contact a rabbit-experienced veterinarian. Unexpected weight changes can sometimes be an early sign of illness.

Important Reminder

This website provides an educational estimate based on the information entered. It cannot replace an examination by a qualified veterinarian. Breed, body condition, medical history, and other factors all influence what is healthiest for an individual rabbit.
