The information entered suggests your rabbit may weigh less than expected for its age and breed size.

What This Means

Being lighter than expected does not always mean a rabbit is unhealthy. However, unexpected low body weight can sometimes be associated with inadequate nutrition, dental problems, parasites, chronic illness, or other medical conditions.

Nutrition

Rabbits should have unlimited grass hay available at all times, along with fresh water every day. Most adult rabbits also benefit from measured rabbit pellets and a variety of rabbit-safe leafy greens. Avoid suddenly changing your rabbit's diet, as abrupt changes can upset their digestive system.

Monitor Your Rabbit

Keep an eye on your rabbit's appetite, droppings, activity level, and body condition. If your rabbit is eating less than normal, losing weight, or seems less active, schedule an appointment with a rabbit-experienced veterinarian as soon as possible.

Veterinary Advice

If your rabbit has become underweight unexpectedly, a veterinarian can check for common causes such as dental disease, digestive disorders, parasites, or other medical conditions. Early treatment often leads to better outcomes.
